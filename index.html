Visitor Guide

The Best of Carolina Beach

Real picks. Local knowledge. No fluff — and zero government spin.

Tacos
Nollie's Taco Joint
So-Cal vibes · Skater soul · Fresh daily

Owned by a retired professional skateboarder, Nollie's brings So-Cal taco culture to CB. Menu items named after skate tricks, tortillas gently grilled, ingredients prepped fresh daily. The pulled pork nachos and mahi tacos are legendary. Outdoor patio, relaxed vibe, 98% approval rating. One of the best things on the island.

TacosOutdoor PatioLocal Favorite
BBQ
Sunny Daze Smokehouse
On the boardwalk · Rooftop views · Live music nights

Bold Carolina BBQ right on the boardwalk with rooftop ocean views. Slow-smoked brisket, pulled pork, smoked wings, sidewinder fries, and six house sauces. The second-floor deck is one of the best spots on the island to eat with a view. Opened in 2024 and already a local staple.

BBQRooftopBoardwalk
Bar & Grill
The Dive Food & Spirits
Since 2008 · Late night · Family owned

Built from scratch in 2008 by Carolina Beach residents, The Dive is a CB original. Voted best wings in Eastern NC — just $1 on Saturdays and Sundays. Great burgers, fish tacos, Greek pitas, and a kitchen open late every night. Music bingo, trivia, karaoke, and DJ on weekends. The real deal.

WingsLate NightLocal
Seafood
Buzz's Crabshack
Boardwalk classic · Low country · Full bar

A retro Carolina Beach boardwalk institution carrying on the legacy of the original Buzz's Roost. Pub food and low country favorites in a cozy bar setting with sidewalk patio seating. Famous crab balls, blue crab cakes, steaks and sandwiches. Frozen rum punch in a souvenir glass in the summer.

SeafoodBoardwalkLow Country
Tiki Bar & Restaurant
SeaWitch Cafe & Tiki Bar
Since 1995 · Live music · Hidden paradise

A Carolina Beach landmark since 1995. A 175-seat restaurant and tiki bar built around lush palm trees, steps from the boardwalk. Family-friendly by day, CB's most popular nightlife venue by night with full production band performances. The steam pot — crab clusters, oysters, shrimp, potatoes and corn — is not to be missed.

Tiki BarLive MusicDog Friendly
Waterfront Dining
Stoked Restaurant
Canal Drive · Dockside · Surf & turf

The only restaurant on the island where you can arrive by boat and dock out back. Surf and turf, pasta, fresh seafood, and a menu built for everyone. Weekend brunch, live music, and a free golf cart pickup for guests. Waterside views, great atmosphere, one of CB's most complete dining experiences.

WaterfrontDocksideBrunch
Burgers
Island Burgers & Bites
In a gas station · Don't judge · Just go

Yes, it's in a gas station. No, that doesn't matter at all. Island Burgers & Bites is one of the most talked-about spots in Carolina Beach — consistently great burgers, friendly service, and the kind of place that surprises everyone who walks in. One of CB's best-kept open secrets.

BurgersHidden GemLocal Favorite
Pizza
Fentoni's Pizzeria
Real pizza · Slices · Stromboli · Calzone

Genuine NYC-style pizza with a crispy crust, great sauce, and proper toppings. Slices, pies, stromboli, calzones — the works. Consistently called the best pizza in Carolina Beach. Dine in, take out, or delivery. The kind of pizza place every beach town needs and CB is lucky to have.

PizzaNYC StyleDelivery
Ice Cream
Squigley's Ice Cream & Treats
Since 1994 · 4,050+ flavors · Get Squiggled

Southeastern NC's most famous ice cream parlor, open since 1994 in a historic pink and white building. The signature Squigley — real vanilla ice cream with your choice of mix-ins squiggled in on the spot — comes in over 4,050 combinations. Cash only. Worth the line. You'll leave with a sticker that says "you've been Squiggled."

Ice CreamSince 1994Cash Only
Oceanfront Bar & Grill
Hurricane Alley
Beach views · Live music · Great tacos

Stunning oceanfront views, cold drinks, and a staff that genuinely cares about their customers. The Jamaican-style tacos are a standout. Live music and special events keep the energy high. One of the best spots on the island to sit with a drink and watch the ocean.

OceanfrontLive MusicFood & Drinks
Boardwalk Bar
Pleasure Island Tavern
The PIT · Boardwalk · Since 2016

Known as "The PIT," Pleasure Island Tavern opened on 4th of July weekend 2016 and has been a boardwalk fixture ever since. A local pub with live music, great food, and the kind of laid-back energy that makes CB feel like CB. Steps from the ocean, regulars who feel like family.

BoardwalkLive MusicLocal Pub
Dive Bar
The Fat Pelican
Voted #1 Dive Bar in NC · Walk-in beer cooler · 400+ beers

Voted the #1 dive bar in North Carolina and top 25 in the nation. Walk into a giant cooler and pick your own beer from over 400 options. Dirt floor outdoor space, eclectic salvaged decor, old school arcade games, and a vibe that can't be manufactured. Completely one of a kind. There is no other bar like this anywhere.

Motel
Dolphin Lane Motel
318 Carolina Beach Ave N · Classic CB stay

A classic Carolina Beach motel that puts you right in the heart of the island. Rooms, efficiencies, and cottages available — the kind of unpretentious, comfortable place that fits the CB spirit perfectly. Everything you need for a great beach trip without the resort price tag. A true island stay.

Iconic Experience
Freeman Park
Drive on the beach · 4WD required · Permit required

Drive your 4WD vehicle right onto the beach at the northern end of the island. Fire pits at night, pelicans by day. Let tires down to ~20 PSI before hitting sand. Bring a pump to reinflate before leaving.

4WDPermitMust Do
Nature
Carolina Beach State Park
Venus flytraps · Hiking · Marsh trails

One of the only places on Earth to see wild Venus flytraps. Miles of trails, a marina, and some of the most beautiful natural scenery on the whole Cape Fear coast.

HikingNatureHidden Gem
Aquarium
Fort Fisher Aquarium
Sharks · Sea turtles · Half-day minimum

One of NC's best aquariums. Sharks, loggerhead sea turtles, native fish. A half-day easily disappears here, especially with kids who have any curiosity at all.

FamilyEducationFort Fisher
Wildlife
Dolphin Spotting on the ICW
Free · Dawn & dusk · Intracoastal Waterway

Head to the ICW at dawn or dusk. Bottlenose dolphins feed in the channel daily. The bridge toward Wilmington is a great free viewing spot most people miss.

WildlifeFreeICW
Freeman Park
4WD on the Beach
Real 4WD only · ~20 PSI · Drive at low tide

AWD won't cut it — you need actual 4WD. Let tires down to ~20 PSI before hitting sand. Drive at low tide. Always bring a pump to reinflate before leaving.

Freeman ParkPro Tip
Timing
Best Sunrise Spot
North end · Before 6am · Zero crowds

Walk or drive to the far north end near Freeman Park before 6am. Wide open sand, not a soul around, and a Carolina sunrise that makes you understand why people never leave.

SunrisePhotographyNorth End
Timing
Beat the Summer Crowds
Late May · Early September · Sweet spot

Late May and early September: warm water, fewer people, lower prices. The shoulder season here is genuinely special — the town feels like it belongs to whoever shows up.

Shoulder SeasonBest Value
Wildlife
Ghost Crab Hunting
After dark · Flashlight · Kids love it

After dark, grab a flashlight and head to the waterline. Ghost crabs scatter when you hit them with light. Fast, translucent, wild. A CB night tradition for every family.

For Locals & Regulars

The Community

This isn't just a vacation spot. It's a town with a pulse, a culture, and people who keep its soul alive year after year.

🎵
Live Music Scene

CB has a real live music culture — beach music, shag dancing, local bands on weeknights. The SeaWitch, Hurricane Alley, and boardwalk bars keep it alive all summer long.

🎣
The Fishing Community

Surf fishermen, offshore captains, and pier regulars who carry irreplaceable knowledge of these waters. One of the most tight-knit communities on the coast.

🐢
Sea Turtle Watch

Local volunteers monitor nesting sea turtles on CB beaches every summer. One of the most beautiful quiet acts of community stewardship on the whole island.

🏄
Surf & Water Culture

A real surf community built over generations. Dawn patrol sessions, ICW paddleboard races, and a deep connection to the water that shapes everyone who lives here.

🎨
Local Artists

Painters, photographers, and craftspeople who draw constant inspiration from the light and landscape here. The boardwalk and local shops are full of their work.

🍽
The Restaurant Community

The restaurant and bar owners here know and support each other. That cooperative spirit makes this a place where small independent businesses genuinely thrive.

Why This Site Exists

We Keep Coming Back.

This site was built out of pure love for this town — its people, its businesses, its boardwalk culture, and the fiercely loyal community that keeps its soul alive year after year.

Whether you're a first-timer or a decades-long regular: you belong here.
